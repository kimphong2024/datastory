§1 — The Architecture
Five layers. One decision spine.

Most insight systems fail because they're organised around research projects rather than around the decisions they serve. This architecture inverts that — every layer feeds toward, and is accountable to, the decisions at its core.

Layer 01

Signal Capture

The raw nerve endings
Where observations enter the system before interpretation. The discipline is to record, not to conclude. Each signal is an atom — tagged but unprocessed.
↓ Click to expand

What enters here

  • Verbatims from research
  • Behavioural data spikes
  • Search trend anomalies
  • Social listening fragments
  • Competitor product changes
  • Cultural moments & events
  • Front-line staff observations
  • Customer service patterns

Required tags

  • Source type: Behavioural, attitudinal, cultural, competitive, category
  • Confidence: Observed / Inferred / Speculative
  • Date captured
  • Capture method: Qual, quant, passive, social, competitive intel
  • Relevance horizon: Immediate / Medium / Long

Rhythm

  • Continuous — always on
  • Low friction entry
  • Weekly review cadence
  • Monthly signal digest

Key discipline

  • Do not interpret at this stage
  • Preserve original language
  • One signal per entry
  • Tag generously, filter later

Layer 02

Pattern Recognition

The connective tissue
Where individual signals get linked. Three signals from different sources pointing in the same direction become a pattern. Patterns carry a convergence score and a trajectory.
↓ Click to expand

Pattern anatomy

  • Name: A descriptive, memorable label
  • Contributing signals: Linked from Layer 1
  • Convergence score: How many independent source types corroborate
  • Trajectory: Emerging → Accelerating → Peaking → Declining
  • First spotted / Last updated

Convergence scoring

  • 1 source type: Hypothesis (monitor)
  • 2 source types: Emerging pattern (investigate)
  • 3+ source types: Robust pattern (act on)
  • A pattern supported by behavioural data + qualitative testimony + cultural trend is far more robust than one from three qualitative studies

Rhythm

  • Bi-weekly synthesis sessions
  • Monthly pattern review
  • Quarterly trajectory update

Key discipline

  • Resist premature closure
  • Look for disconfirming evidence
  • Update trajectories, don't just add
  • Name patterns vividly
Layer 03

Insight Crystallisation

The interpretive layer
Where patterns get meaning. Every insight must articulate the tension it reveals — tensions are what make insights actionable. This is where "what's happening" becomes "what it means for people and why."
↓ Click to expand

Insight anatomy

  • The observation: What we see happening
  • The underlying driver: Why this is happening (human truth)
  • The tension: The unresolved pull between competing needs, desires, or realities
  • Supporting patterns: Linked from Layer 2
  • Confidence & maturity level

Tension archetypes

  • Want vs. Constraint: "People want X but are blocked by Y"
  • Promise vs. Reality: "The category says A but delivers B"
  • Identity vs. Behaviour: "People see themselves as X but act as Y"
  • Old norm vs. Emerging norm: "The established way is X but the pull is toward Y"

Rhythm

  • Monthly insight workshops
  • Quarterly insight library refresh
  • Annual tension landscape review

Key discipline

  • An insight without a tension is just an observation
  • Test: could a competitor use this insight differently?
  • Write for a strategist, not a researcher
  • Track tension evolution over time

Layer 04

Implication Mapping

The strategic translation
Where insight meets business context. The same insight can have radically different implications depending on the business's position, capabilities, and ambitions. This layer asks: given what we now understand, what options does this open or close?
↓ Click to expand

Implication anatomy

  • Source insight: Linked from Layer 3
  • Decision domain: Brand, innovation, pricing, channel, comms, CX
  • Strategic options: What this opens
  • Strategic risks: What this closes or threatens
  • Urgency: Act now / Plan for / Monitor

Decision domains

  • Brand positioning & identity
  • Innovation pipeline & NPD
  • Pricing architecture
  • Channel & distribution strategy
  • Communication & media
  • Customer experience design
  • Organisational capability

Rhythm

  • Monthly implication workshops with strategy
  • Quarterly roadmap alignment
  • Ad hoc for urgent signals

Key discipline

  • Always name the trade-off
  • Present options, not conclusions
  • Map to specific business decisions
  • Distinguish between implications for us vs. for category
Layer 05

Decision Activation

The spine itself
The registry of actual business decisions — past, pending, and anticipated. Each decision carries its own anatomy: trigger, evidence, tensions navigated, bet placed, and outcome observed. This is where the loop closes.
↓ Click to expand

Decision record

  • The trigger: Why now?
  • The question: What specifically are we deciding?
  • Evidence base: Linked insights & convergence scores
  • Tensions navigated: What trade-offs were accepted?
  • The bet: What hypothesis underlies this choice?

Feedback loop

  • Expected outcome: What should happen if we're right, by when?
  • Feedback triggers: What signals would tell us this is working or failing?
  • Actual outcome: What happened?
  • New signals generated: → flows back to Layer 1

Rhythm

  • Decision logged at point of commitment
  • Monthly feedback trigger review
  • Quarterly outcome audit
  • Annual decision quality review

Key discipline

  • Record the decision before you know the outcome
  • Be explicit about what you're betting on
  • Distinguish "the decision was wrong" from "the execution was poor"
  • Close the loop — always

§2 — Connective Mechanisms
What makes this connected,
not just organised.
⤮

Threading

Any element at any layer can link to any element at any other layer. A signal can connect directly to a decision if urgent. An implication can reach back to challenge an old pattern. The system isn't a funnel — it's a web with a gravitational centre.

⇌

Tension Tracking

Tensions are the through-line. A tension identified in Layer 3 might persist for years — new signals adding nuance, new patterns shifting its shape, multiple decisions attempting to resolve it. This reveals whether the business addresses root dynamics or just treats symptoms.

◴

Temporal Tagging

Every element carries a time dimension — not just "when captured" but "what's the relevance horizon?" A cultural macro-trend has a 3–5 year window. A competitive move, 6 months. A behavioural signal in digital data might be a 2–3 month leading indicator. Filter by decision urgency.

§3 — Decision Anatomy
Eight elements of every
business decision.

The outcome of a decision generates new signals, which may reinforce or challenge existing patterns, which updates the insight base for the next decision. The system breathes.

01

The Trigger

Why is this decision being made now? Market pressure, opportunity window, internal initiative, or crisis.

02

The Question

What specifically are we deciding? Framing the question well is half the answer.

03

The Evidence Base

Linked insights, patterns, and signals — with their confidence and convergence scores.

04

Tensions Navigated

What trade-offs does this decision involve? What are we choosing not to optimise for?

05

The Bet

What are we choosing, and what is the underlying hypothesis about why it will work?

06

Expected Outcome

What should happen if we're right — and by when? Make it specific and measurable.

07

Feedback Triggers

What signals would tell us this is working or failing? Pre-commit to what you'll monitor.

08

Actual Outcome

What happened? New signals flow back to Layer 1. The loop closes. The system learns.
